I am Excited. Excited to start. I don’t know how long the momentum will last but I think I should give this a go. What is this? Creating this website. Expressing myself through code. Will it amount to much? Probably not. But what do I have to lose? Mostly Motivation. I guess.

I want so many things for this site. I don’t want it to be a WordPress site. I don’t want it to be easy or beautiful. I want it to be an expression of who I am. Why? Because this whole thing is futile anyway. I am unknown. Just a guy with a laptop sitting in the middle of a swamp. I am not Mark Zuckerburg or Jeff Bezos. I have barely maintained 300 followers on Instagram and spend most of my money on Amazon. What I have thought is the opportunity to make something. Anything. To give it life and then let it sit dormant like the millions of other blogs out there.

You know what really inspired me to write this?

I work at Amazon in a Fulfillment Center; I got certified in a bootcamp in Front End Development through that Job. What am I doing with that Certificate? Becoming a Data analyst for the Learning Department at that site. This job is to literally just be a jack of all trades coding and hacking together quick solutions for the most mundane of problems. BUT I LOVE IT!

I spend hours upon hours every week trudging through half-baked internal wiki pages and getting lost in ultra technical documents that I have no business reading. Just looking for anything that can help me with the basic needs of my job, and I found this guy on the internal stack overflow page answering so many of my questions that were providentially asked by the other people stumbling through the dark just like me. He was wearing a Bow Tie and instead of just supplying code, this man was diving in and creating Libraries for the superfluous and creating Modules for the Obscure and pushing for innovations in the ways that are overlooked. I appreciated it so much.

I had to look this guy up. He had left. I tried to add him on LinkedIn, nothing. But I did see he had a blog. What was awesome though was he had maintained it for 7 years!! I was astonished. What perseverance! I read some of his work and it was full of passion projects and advice to people like me and I decided right there. I was going to document my Journey too. WHY NOT. If I could be so inspired by someone relatively unknown then there was a slight chance that I could do the same for someone else.

 

So Here it goes. I’m off.
